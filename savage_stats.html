When players have finished the MSQ, spent all their gil at the casino,
fished all the fish in the sea... what better is there to do than raid?
But, what even is raiding?



Raiding generally refers to endgame content. After completing the Main Story Quests, players can unlock the raids for that expansion, and in turn, unlock the savage difficulty of them after clearing the raids' at normal difficulty.
Additionally, every few patches, a new Ultimate raid is released. Ultimate raids have the most complicated mechanics in the game, and often requires party members to verbally communicate and be exceptionally coordinated to succeed. The act of making progress (progging) and playing these Ultimate and Savage raids is called raiding.

Given the difficulty of raiding, from the intricate mechanics to the strict DPS requirements, there are often "preferred" Jobs and party setups. Many teamp compositions will prefer Jobs that have a higher damage potential or exceptional utility, but which Jobs do the most damage? I gathered data about the top damage dealers for four Savage raids in the Pandaemonium series. I used FFlogs, a site to upload raid battle data for combat analysis, to get this information.

The chart is based on rDPS, a DPS measurement that accounts for the buffs some Jobs can give. In other words, a player's rDPS consists of their DPS excluding the damage gained from others' buffs, including the damage the player gave to others through their buffs. It best shows how much damage was contributed by a player. Further explanation can be found at this FFLogs page.

To adjust the view of this chart, highlight the section you want to enlarge,
and the window will resize to that selection.
This x-axis represents DPS values.

From the chart, it is clear that Melee Jobs have the highest average damage. Dragoon is a straightforward in playstyle and is relatively strong, with some DRG outliers dealing the most damage overall. Monk is consistent in dealing high damage, but is fast-paced and may be difficult to master (and reach the high damage ceiling MNK has). The Physical Ranged and Caster Jobs perform adequately. An interesting point is Dancer, whose upper damage range is quite high. This is likely due to the playstyle of the Dancer Job: it revolves around buffing ally damage. Therefore, if a player's party members are already doing high damage, with the rDPS calculation, the buffs Dancer grants will significantly boost their own rDPS.


In North American Worlds, the distribution of Jobs (as explored in the previous page) is relatively even. Then, in a vaccuum, it would make sense that all Worlds are equally active in the raiding scene; is this true?
The below chart displays the percentage of players who have cleared Abyssos: The Eighth Circle (Savage) (P8S), the final raid battle for the second section of Pandaemonium, by World. The percentage is calculated by dividing the number of people who have cleared by the total number of active players in that World.


There is a clear difference in P8S clear rates across Data Centers. Aether Worlds have the highest clear rates, followed by Primal, then Crystal and Dynamis. As seen on the Populations page, the only significantly small Data Center is the newly added Dynamis.
Therefore, the notion that Crystal is more of a casual Data Center seems true. Crystal, despite being the same size (or slightly larger) than Aether and Primal, has the has similar clear rates to Dynamis, a new and small Data Center. Because player numbers do not seem to be affecting this rate, it can be assumed that Crystal is indeed a more casual Data Center and places less emphasis on raiding.
